Playstation 4

Explore our definitive ranking of the greatest PlayStation 4 games ever made, as voted by users, Metacritic ratings, and our own experts. Discover your next favorite title, from action-packed adventures to immersive RPGs, all ranked for your convenience.

🏆 1- The Last Of Us Part II

The Last of Us Part 2 is considered a masterpiece and won numerous Game of the Year awards. Its engaging story explores themes such as revenge, loss, and the human condition, with well-developed characters that players become invested in. The game features innovative gameplay mechanics, such as the ability to go prone and crawl through tight spaces, which adds a new level of strategy to combat encounters. It emphasizes stealth and resource management, creating a tense and challenging experience. Additionally, the stunning visuals, realistic character animations, and immersive sound design all contribute to the game's immersive and emotionally impactful world.

2- Uncharted 4: A Thief's End

Uncharted 4: A Thief's End is a thrilling action-adventure game that follows retired treasure hunter Nathan Drake as he embarks on a dangerous journey with his long-lost brother in search of a pirate treasure. With stunning graphics, intense combat, and memorable set-piece moments, Uncharted 4 is a must-play game that expertly blends storytelling, character development, and satisfying gameplay.

3- Bloodborne

Bloodborne is widely regarded as one of the best games in the "Soulsborne" series for several reasons. One of its standout features is its unique setting, which takes place in the Gothic-inspired city of Yharnam. The city's dark and foreboding atmosphere contributes greatly to the overall feeling of dread and horror that permeates the game. In addition to the setting, Bloodborne's combat system is fast-paced and aggressive, encouraging players to stay on the offensive and dodge and parry enemy attacks while delivering their own devastating blows. This differs from previous Souls games, which had a more methodical and defensive approach to combat. Bloodborne's bosses are also a standout feature, as they are some of the most challenging and memorable in the series. Each boss has its own unique moveset and requires a different approach to defeat, which keeps the game feeling fresh and exciting. Finally, the game's lore is deep and complex, with a rich history and mythology that is slowly revealed to players through item descriptions, environmental clues, and NPC interactions. The story is full of mystery and intrigue, and many players enjoy piecing together the various threads to uncover its secrets. All of these elements combine to create a well-crafted and immersive experience that has solidified Bloodborne's place as one of the best games in the Soulsborne series.

4- Ghost of Tsushima

Ghost of Tsushima is a highly acclaimed action-adventure video game developed by Sucker Punch Productions and released in 2020 for the PlayStation 4 and PlayStation 5 consoles. The game is set in the late 13th century on the Japanese island of Tsushima during the first Mongol invasion of Japan, and follows the story of Jin Sakai, a samurai warrior who must protect his home from the invading forces.
The game is praised for its stunning visuals, immersive open world, and engaging combat system that allows players to choose between stealth and direct confrontation. It also features a rich story that explores themes of honor, duty, and sacrifice, as well as the cultural clashes between the samurai and the Mongols.
Overall, Ghost of Tsushima is considered a masterpiece of game design, offering players a compelling and emotionally resonant experience that is both entertaining and thought-provoking.

It's an exciting game that is fun to play both in short bursts or for hours on end.

6- Animal Crossing: New Horizons

The latest installment of Animal Crossing is perfect for anybody searching for a form of escape. The game is delightful and great for fans of The Sims or Stardew Valley. It’s simple, friendly, and packed full of customization options. You have full control of your character’s appearance, their home, and even the geography of the island that you live on.
